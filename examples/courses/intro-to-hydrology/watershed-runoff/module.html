1 · What is a watershed?

A watershed (also called a catchment or drainage basin) is the area of land that drains to a single outlet — a stream gauge, a lake, or a river mouth. Every point on land belongs to exactly one watershed.

The outer boundary is the divide: a ridge line along which any raindrop on one side eventually flows one way, and a drop on the other side flows the opposite way. The divide follows the highest topographic points surrounding the catchment.

🏔️
The Divide

A continuous ridge line forming the outer boundary. Defined entirely by topography — extractable from a DEM without any surveying.

Key fact The divide is a line of zero cross-divide slope. Water on the divide is in unstable equilibrium — it could go either way.
🌊
The Outlet

The single point where all water leaving the watershed is measured. Change the outlet location → change the watershed boundary.

Key fact Watersheds are nested: every tributary has its own sub-catchment. They stack like Russian dolls up to the largest basin.

3 · How topography decides where water goes — the D8 algorithm

A computer extracts the watershed by applying one rule to every cell in a DEM: water flows to whichever of the 8 neighbouring cells is lowest. This is the D8 algorithm (eight-direction flow).

D8 in 3 steps
  1. Look at 8 neighbours (N, NE, E, SE, S, SW, W, NW).
  2. Find the steepest drop — the neighbour with the greatest elevation difference.
  3. Follow that path downhill until the edge of the DEM or a sink.
From D8 to watershed All cells whose D8 path passes through your outlet form the watershed. The divide is the outer edge of that set.

5 · Checkpoint

Three questions — pass all three to mark this module complete and unlock DEM → TWI.

1 · The watershed divide is defined by…

Stream channels are inside the watershed — they're where water converges, not diverges.
Watershed boundaries are purely topographic; they have nothing to do with land ownership.

2 · In the D8 algorithm, a cell's flow direction points toward…

Averaging doesn't give a direction — you need the single steepest downslope path.
D8 uses only local elevation differences, not global distances to streams.

3 · Moving the stream gauge 10 km upstream would…

The watershed is defined relative to the outlet — change the outlet and the boundary changes.
Moving upstream cuts off the area between old and new gauge — it makes the watershed smaller.
